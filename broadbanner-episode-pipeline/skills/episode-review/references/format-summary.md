# Summary + Bullets Review Format

Used by shows with topical/news discussion formats where the review is a tight summary with actionable takeaways. Currently used by: **Notes of the Week (NOTW)**, **Palantalk (PALAN)**, **Firebrand Report (FR)**, **Epistemic Warfare (EW)**, **Time for Life (TFL)**.

## Structure

The output is a single markdown file with these sections in order:

### 1. SEO Title + Metadata

```
**SEO Title:** [title per format from series metadata]
**Subtitle:** [1 sentence, active voice, ≤250 characters]
```

**NOTW title format:** `Notes Of The Week | E# - <short title> - YYYY/MM/DD`
**PALAN title format:** `Palantalk | E# - <short title> - YYYY/MM/DD`
**FR title format:** `Firebrand Report | E# - <short title> - YYYY/MM/DD`
**EW title format:** `Epistemic Warfare | E# - <short title> - YYYY/MM/DD`
**TFL title format:** `Time for Life | E# - <short title> - YYYY/MM/DD`

Short title rules: 2-7 words, concrete nouns/verbs, no clickbait, no rhetorical questions.

### 2. Summary Paragraph

Write **1 paragraph** of **4-6 sentences** in active voice.

Must cover:

- What happened in the episode
- Why it matters
- What listeners can do with it

No vague phrasing. No "This was an important conversation." Be specific about topics, claims, and consequences.

### 3. Key Takeaways

Write **3-5 bullet points**, each **1-2 sentences**.

Rules:

- Make each actionable — use verbs like "read," "verify," "compare," "track," "contact," "document"
- Avoid platitudes
- Maintain variety in structure
- If the episode is exploratory rather than prescriptive, convert takeaways into "what to watch" or "what to verify next"

### 4. People, Orgs, and Terms Mentioned

Three sub-sections, each with 2-5 entries:

**People:** `- [Name] — [why relevant, 1 line]`
**Organizations / Programs:** `- [Org] — [why relevant, 1 line]`
**Terms / Concepts:** `- [Term] — [plain-language definition, 1 line]`

Only include entities that are meaningful to understanding the episode. Skip incidental mentions.

### 5. Sources & References (optional)

Only include if sources were explicitly referenced. 2-5 maximum. Don't overlink.

### 6. Social Distribution Copy

- **Substack blurb:** 1-2 paragraphs
- **Bluesky post:** ≤300 characters
- **YouTube description:** 2-3 short paragraphs

## Writing constraints

- Active voice throughout
- Concrete nouns and verbs — no vague abstractions
- "This episode shows..." not "This was an important conversation..."
- No "negative clause, but positive response" pattern overuse
- Paragraphs: 2-5 sentences
- Lists: 2-5 items
- Use first names for contributors unless formal attribution required
- Never fabricate quotes, timestamps, or sources
- Mark anything missing with `[missing]`
