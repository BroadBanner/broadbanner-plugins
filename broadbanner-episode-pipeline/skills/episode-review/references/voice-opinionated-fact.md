# Editorial Voice: Opinionated + Fact-Driven

Used by shows where the hosts take clear positions grounded in verifiable claims. The review should reflect the editorial stance without softening it, but every opinion must be traceable to something said or sourced in the episode.

## Tone

- **Active and direct.** "Nick and Shane argue that..." not "It was suggested that..."
- **First-person editorial perspective is allowed.** The review can say "This matters because..." as a statement of editorial position, not hedged with "some may think."
- **Assertive, not aggressive.** Take the position. Don't yell it.

## Sourcing stance

- Claims from the episode are reported as claims: "Paro argues X, citing Y."
- When the hosts cite data or sources, name them. When they editorialize, attribute the editorial: "Shane frames this as..."
- Do not launder host opinions as neutral facts. Attribution is the mechanism.

## What to avoid

- Hedging language: "It could be argued," "Some might say," "There are those who believe"
- False balance: Don't introduce counterpoints the episode didn't raise just to seem neutral
- Emotional escalation: Match the hosts' energy, don't exceed it
- Vague moral claims: "This is wrong" without the hosts' reasoning attached

## Sentence construction

- Lead with the actor: "Nick points out..." "The data shows..."
- Prefer short declarative sentences for key claims, longer compound sentences for context
- Use imperative mood for calls to action: "Read the report." "Track this bill."
