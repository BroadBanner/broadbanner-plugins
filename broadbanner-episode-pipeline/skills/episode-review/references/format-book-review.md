# Book Review Format

Used by shows structured around reading and discussing a specific book across multiple episodes. Currently used by: **Anti-Fascist Book Club (AFBC)**.

## Structure

The output is a single markdown file with these sections in order:

### 1. SEO Title + Metadata

```
**SEO Title:** [title per format from series metadata]
**Subtitle:** [1 sentence, active voice, ≤250 characters]
```

**AFBC title format:** `Anti Fascist Book Club | <book name> - <short title> - YYYY/MM/DD`

Short title rules: 2-7 words, captures the episode's focus within the book, no clickbait.

### 2. Block Quote

Extract 1 powerful direct quote from the discussion — either a panelist's insight or a passage from the book read aloud. Format:

```
> "[quote text]"
>
> _\~ [Speaker Name] \~_
```

Rules: Must be an actual quote from the transcript. If quoting the book, attribute to the author. Choose the quote that best captures the episode's central argument or the book's thesis as discussed.

### 3. Review Body

- H3 header: `### Book In Review`
- 3-5 paragraphs of narrative body (3-6 sentences each)
- First paragraph: what section of the book was covered, what the key arguments are, and how the panel engaged with them
- Middle paragraphs: the substance — what claims the book makes, how the panel tested or extended them, what connections were drawn to current events or prior readings
- Final paragraph: what this episode adds to the series arc, what readers/listeners should carry forward
- No sub-headers within the body — it flows as continuous narrative

### 4. Books Mentioned

List every book discussed with links. Format:

```
- *[Book Title]* by [Author] — [link]
```

Link preference: publisher direct > independent bookstore > online thrift. Avoid large tech company bookstores. If no acceptable link exists: "Publisher link unavailable."

Always include the primary book being discussed. Include secondary references only if they were substantively discussed (not just name-dropped).

### 5. Sources & References (optional)

Only include if sources beyond the book were explicitly referenced in the episode — articles, reports, historical events with specific citations. 2-5 maximum. Don't overlink.

### 6. Social Distribution Copy

- **Substack blurb:** 1-2 paragraphs focusing on the book and what the episode reveals about it
- **Bluesky post:** ≤300 characters
- **YouTube description:** 2-3 short paragraphs

## Writing constraints

- Active voice throughout
- Engage with the book's ideas, not just what was said about them
- "The book argues..." "The panel pushes back on..." "This chapter reveals..."
- Connect the episode to the book's larger thesis when possible
- Paragraphs: 3-6 sentences
- Lists: 2-5 items
- Use first names for panelists unless formal attribution required
- Never fabricate quotes, timestamps, or sources
- Mark anything missing with `[missing]`
