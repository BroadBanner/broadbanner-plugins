# Narrative Review Format

Used by shows with long-form interview/discussion formats where the review reads as a cohesive essay. Currently used by: **Intelligent Masculinity (IM)**, **Chronically Illing Out (CIO)**, **Diogenes Club (DC)**.

## Structure

The output is a single markdown file with these sections in order:

### 1. SEO Title + Metadata

```
**SEO Title:** [title per format from series metadata]
**Pages H2:** [one sentence — see show-specific rules below]
**Subtitle:** [1 sentence, active voice, ≤250 characters]
```

**IM title format:** `Intelligent Masculinity | With <Guest Name> - YYYY/MM/DD`
**IM Pages H2 format:** `Join in with Nick Paro and [Guest] for a better humaning conversation [about/focused on/centered on] [key topics].`

**CIO/DC title format:** `[Series Name] | E# - <short title> - YYYY/MM/DD`
**CIO/DC** use Subtitle only — no Pages H2.

### 2a. Block Quote

Extract 1 powerful direct quote from the guest (IM) or the discussion (CIO, DC). Format:

```
> "[quote text]"
>
> _\~ [Speaker Name] \~_
```

Rules: Must be an actual quote from the transcript. Never fabricate. Choose the quote that best captures the guest's core philosophy or the episode's central argument.

### 2b. Review Body

**IM structure:**

- H3 header: `### Masculinity In Review`
- 1-paragraph introduction (4-6 sentences): what happened, what it means, what listeners can do
- 3-4 key takeaway paragraphs (4-6 sentences each): what is the takeaway, who is involved, why it matters
- 1-paragraph closing (3-5 sentences): who the guest is, what the interview covered, a powerful closing line on how this guest expands the series
- No sub-headers within the body — it flows as continuous narrative
- Close with: `\~ [Nick Paro](host-url)`

**CIO structure:**

- H3 header: `### Chronic Illness In Review`
- 1-paragraph introduction (4-6 sentences)
- 3-4 key takeaway paragraphs (3-6 sentences each): focused on lived experience, practical knowledge, and destigmatization
- 1-paragraph closing (3-5 sentences)
- No sub-headers within the body

**DC structure:**

- H3 header: `### Diogenes In Review`
- 1-paragraph introduction (4-6 sentences)
- 3-4 key takeaway paragraphs (3-6 sentences each): focused on philosophical arguments, cultural critique, and civic implications
- 1-paragraph closing (3-5 sentences)
- No sub-headers within the body

### 3. Sources & References (optional)

Only include if sources were explicitly referenced in the episode. 2-5 maximum. Don't overlink.

### 4. Social Distribution Copy

- **Substack blurb:** 1-2 paragraphs
- **Bluesky post:** ≤300 characters
- **YouTube description:** 2-3 short paragraphs

## Writing constraints

- Active voice throughout
- Concrete nouns and verbs — no vague abstractions
- "This episode shows..." not "This was an important conversation..."
- No "negative clause, but positive response" pattern overuse
- Paragraphs: 2-5 sentences
- Lists: 2-5 items
- Use first names for contributors unless formal attribution required
- Never fabricate quotes, timestamps, or sources
- Mark anything missing with `[missing]`
